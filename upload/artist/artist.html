Vincent van Gogh

Post-Impressionist Master

About Me

Vincent van Gogh (1853-1890) was a Dutch Post-Impressionist painter known for his bold colors, swirling brushstrokes, and emotional intensity. A troubled genius, he struggled with mental illness throughout his life, yet produced some of the most iconic paintings in Western art history. His works, including "Starry Night," "Sunflowers," and "The Bedroom," continue to inspire and captivate audiences around the world.

Artist Statement

"I dream my painting and I paint my dream." - Vincent van Gogh (In a letter to his brother Theo, 1888)

Van Gogh's art was often an expression of his inner turmoil and his yearning for beauty and connection. He believed in the power of color and brushwork to evoke emotions and convey his unique perspective on the world.

Featured Work

The Starry Night (1889)

A swirling masterpiece depicting a vibrant night sky over a sleepy village.

Sunflowers (1888)

A series of vibrant paintings showcasing sunflowers in various stages of bloom, symbolizing hope and resilience.

The Bedroom (1888)

A depiction of his simple, yet lived-in bedroom in Arles, France, conveying a sense of intimacy and personal space.
